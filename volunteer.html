Africa School Assistance Project (ASAP) is a non-profit organisation that builds schools in rural Tanzanian villages and each summer leads groups of volunteers to work on the schools. ASAP is currently seeking volunteers for school building trips in June 2018. They will be working in a rural area outside of Arusha, Tanzania to build, expand and enhance school facilities for school children of the area. They welcome adults and children alike—anyone with an adventurous spirit and a big heart! Volunteers will spend their days working on building the schools, and will spend their evenings under some of the most stunning stars visible from anywhere on earth! learn more

Build Africa works to identify vulnerable government and community schools that are already in existence. Using that unique model, they then address the needs of children at home, at school and in the community - working together with parents and teachers to overcome the barriers of poverty and inequality that can stand in their children's way. They help parents to earn a secure living, communities to value education and tackle discrimination against girls and marginalised children so that no child is left behind. They construct and renovate classrooms, libraries and important facilities, as well as improve access to hygienic toilets and clean drinking water - creating a safe and engaging environment for children. They provide important educational materials and textbooks, train teachers and give them the professional support they need to inspire their students. learn more

A Glimer of Hope Foundation fights poverty in rural Ethiopia with a holistic approach to change. They apply 100% of donations directly to projects that lift up families and help create thriving communities. They help lift women and children out of extreme poverty in rural Ethiopia, since 2000, they have helped more than 5 million people change their lives. Glimmer believes the best way to affect sustainable change is to tackle all of the unique areas that perpetuate poverty. Rather than focus on a single issue like water or education, Glimmer’s unique model of integrated development means that they work across many different sectors to bring deep, lasting change. learn more

Action Africa helps children in Sub-Saharan Africa lead healthier, more productive lives. Their work supports education, health, early preparation against pandemics, safety, particularly for orphans and girls, who face greater risk for violence, neglect and illness. Every thirty seconds, a child dies of a treatable disease, complications from HIV/AIDS, or hunger. Since 2000, Action Africa has worked with local communities to meet these challenges through diverse on-the-ground programs that focus on the issues of health, education, economic self-sufficiency and human rights. With a sponsor’s $12 monthly contribution, a child is able to go to school and receive nutritional and health support. learn more

Fistula Foundation helps women with obstetric fistula, a wrenching childbirth injury that leaves over a million women incontinent and, too often, cast out from their communities. They provide life-transforming surgeries to end the suffering and restore women’s health. They fund fistula surgeries and fund the very best organizations helping in the places where need for fistula treatment is greatest, partnering with the most respected, productive and effective hospitals and local doctors. In the last four years, they’ve grown from focusing exclusively on Ethiopia to funding 42 projects in 18 additional countries. learn more

Green Belt Movement International provides income to hundreds of thousands of Kenyan women and families through tree planting. They also mobilize for women’s rights, good governance, civic empowerment, and the environment. The act of planting a tree is helping women throughout Africa become stewards of the natural environment. To date, more than 51 million trees have been planted across Kenya to protect critical watersheds, restore thousands of acres of indigenous forests, and empower thousands of women and their families. These women and their families are now living healthier and more productive lives and are standing up for their rights and those of their communities. learn more

Solar Sister helps recruit training and mentoring women to run small solar light and clean-stove businesses in Uganda, Nigeria and Tanzania in order to eradicate energy poverty in communities without electricity. We recruit, train and support local women to become clean-energy entrepreneurs, selling and delivering solar lights and efficient cookstoves to those who need it most. Solar Sister provides the backbone of women’s enterprise – with training, logistical support, and ongoing mentoring – so that any woman can build a business and transform life for herself and her neighbors. Every donation helps us launch the next Solar Sister entrepreneur. Since 2010, Solar Sister has built a network of over 2,200 entrepreneurs and has brought clean energy access to over 675,000 people across Uganda, Nigeria and Tanzania. learn more

Village Health Works provides quality, community-driven medical care and local development projects--particularly for women and children. They deliver world-class, community-driven medical care and local development initiatives. To treat the root causes of disease and illness, VHW combines quality health care with development initiatives identified by and implemented with members of the community, including projects addressing sustainable agriculture, education, skills development, and gender-based violence prevention. learn more

Worldwide Fistula Fund restores and protects the health and dignity of the world's most vulnerable women and girls by healing and preventing devastating childbirth injuries through surgery, education & vocational skills, capacity building doctor training and community advocacy. omen who suffer from fistula are often shunned by their families and communities. To help them rebuild their lives, Worldwide Fistula Foundation provides education and vocational skills training, literacy and health classes and more. They support medical and community training programs. Medical staff, including physicians, receive obstetrical and gynecological training in partnership with Mekelle University in Ethiopia. With their partners in Ethiopia, they support a fellowship program to train Ethiopian gynecologists in advanced pelvic floor medicine and reconstructive pelvic surgery. Community health advocates are also trained in fistula awareness and risk factors and to encourage local families to give birth in health centers.learn more
